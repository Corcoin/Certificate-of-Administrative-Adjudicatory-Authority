Texas Administrative Adjudication Guide

This white paper provides an overview for professionals seeking to operate as a private or contract Administrative Law Judge (ALJ) or adjudicator in Texas. It covers procedural authority, best practices, and recommended rates for independent adjudicators.

1. Role of a Texas Administrative Law Judge

A Texas ALJ presides over hearings conducted under state regulatory frameworks. Duties typically include:

  • Managing contested hearings from initiation to final resolution
  • Ruling on admissibility and weight of evidence under Texas rules
  • Conducting sworn testimony and cross-examination
  • Maintaining the integrity of the administrative record
  • Issuing written findings of fact and final decisions

2. Leveraging Non-JD Experience

Non-lawyers with regulatory and adjudication experience may serve in private or agency-appointed ALJ roles in Texas. Qualifications often include:

  • Regulatory knowledge in the relevant field (labor, environmental, HR, etc.)
  • Prior hearing officer or adjudicator experience
  • Proven ability to render impartial decisions

3. Payment Ranges

Typical compensation for contract Texas ALJ services depends on case type and complexity:

  • Hourly Rates: $250–$500/hour
  • Per Case: $1,500–$5,000
  • Full-Day Retainer: $5,000+

Rates should be documented in engagement letters or contracts, and NDAs are recommended for confidentiality.

4. Best Practices

  • Maintain neutrality and impartiality at all times
  • Document all proceedings thoroughly
  • Ensure compliance with Texas administrative law standards
  • Use NDAs to protect confidential case information
  • Leverage credentials and prior case experience for marketing and client trust

5. How to Promote Yourself

  • Use a professional website and credential display
  • Provide verifiable certificates and QR codes
  • Network with law firms, HR departments, and regulatory agencies
  • Offer consulting and expert witness services alongside adjudication
